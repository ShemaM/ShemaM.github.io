The Silent Genocide in Eastern DRC

A Global Discussion on Leadership and Peace

Massive Turnout at Importance Leadership’s Online Workshop

Event Overview

On March 1, 2025, Importance Leadership hosted a powerful online discussion on the ongoing crisis in eastern DRC, drawing youth from across the world—Australia, Canada, the USA, Kenya, DRC, Rwanda, South Sudan, Burundi, and beyond. This urgent meeting aimed to share insights, dispel misinformation, and raise awareness about the true nature of the conflict. The workshop was graced by young researchers with deep knowledge of the region's history.

Workshop Focus

The discussion explored the persistent instability in Eastern DRC, highlighting:

  • Mineral Wealth: The abundance of cobalt, cassiterite, gold, and pyrochlore attracting foreign exploitation.
  • Political Instability: Displacement and marginalization of Congolese Tutsi communities.

Key Takeaways & Solutions

  • Changing the narrative—Recognizing the role of global players.
  • Promoting inclusive governance and equal citizenship.
  • Encouraging regional collaboration through the AU and EAC.
  • Managing minerals for local economic benefit.
  • African-led military and diplomatic strategies for peace.

We call upon the youth to become champions of peace—leveraging innovation, advocacy, and leadership to promote social cohesion. By embracing diversity, respecting all cultures, and standing firmly against violence, we can create a brighter, more inclusive Africa.
